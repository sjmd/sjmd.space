Social justice dogma?
Here's what we mean:

The social justice dogma is the set of beliefs, stances, and acceptable actions laid down by the authorities within the social justice movement that we hold as incontrovertibly true.
Definition by Sam Killermann from his 2017 article I can’t stop thinking about the “Social Justice Dogma,” or keeping quiet.
Social Justice, Minus Dogma is a space for us to question the dogma, investigate the ways we might be creating barriers for ourselves, and explore paths to living social justice.
